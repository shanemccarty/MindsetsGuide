10  Scoring Guide & References

This appendix provides the complete scoring guide for all measures in this ebook, including subscale item keys, response scales, and academic references.

10.1 Scoring Guide

10.1.1 Chapter 3: Regulatory Focus

Regulatory Focus Questionnaire (RFQ; Higgins et al., 2001)

Subscale Items Reverse-Scored Scale
Promotion Focus 1(R), 2, 3, 4(R), 5, 6(R) 1, 4, 6 1–5 (Never to Very Often)
Prevention Focus 7(R), 8(R), 9, 10(R), 11 7, 8, 10 1–5 (Never to Very Often)

Health Regulatory Focus Scale (HRFS; Ferrer et al., 2017)

Subscale Items Reverse-Scored Scale
Health Promotion Focus 1–6 None 1–6 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
Health Prevention Focus 7–12 None 1–6 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

Prosocial Regulatory Focus Scale (McCarty et al., 2026)

Subscale Items Reverse-Scored Scale
Promoting Care 1–5 None 1–6 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
Preventing Harm 6–10 None 1–6 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

10.1.2 Chapter 4: Health Literacy

Health Literacy Scale (8 items)

Subscale Items Reverse-Scored Scale
Health Literacy (composite) 1–8 None 1–5 (Very Bad to Very Good)

Note: Responses of “I do not make use of this kind of information” are treated as missing.

10.1.3 Chapter 5: Psychological Beliefs

FAD-Plus (Paulhus & Carey, 2011)

Subscale Items Scale
Free Will 4, 8, 12, 16, 21, 23, 26 1–5 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
Scientific Determinism 2, 6, 10, 14, 18, 22, 24 1–5
Fatalistic Determinism 1, 5, 9, 13, 17 1–5
Unpredictability 3, 7, 11, 15, 19, 20, 25, 27 1–5

Nutrition Self-Efficacy Scale (Schwarzer & Renner, 2000)

Subscale Items Scale
Nutrition Self-Efficacy 1–5 1–4 (Very Uncertain to Very Certain)

Physical Exercise Self-Efficacy Scale (Schwarzer & Renner, 2000)

Subscale Items Scale
Exercise Self-Efficacy 1–5 1–4 (Very Uncertain to Very Certain)

Health Information Avoidance — General (7 scenarios)

Subscale Items Scale
General Avoidance (composite) 1–7 1–4 (Definitely Don’t Want to Know to Definitely Want to Know)

Note: Higher scores indicate more information seeking (less avoidance).

Health Information Avoidance — Depression (8 items)

Subscale Items Reverse-Scored Scale
Depression Avoidance 1, 2, 3(R), 4, 5(R), 6, 7(R), 8(R) 3, 5, 7, 8 1–7 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

Note: After reverse-scoring, higher scores indicate more avoidance.

10.1.4 Chapter 6: Health Attributions & Morality

Moral Foundations Questionnaire (Graham et al., 2011)

Foundation Block 1 Items (Relevance) Block 2 Items (Agreement) Scales
Harm/Care 1, 7 1, 7 B1: 1–5; B2: 1–6
Fairness/Reciprocity 2, 8 2, 8 B1: 1–5; B2: 1–6
Ingroup/Loyalty 3, 9 12, 9 B1: 1–5; B2: 1–6
Authority/Respect 4, 10 4, 10 B1: 1–5; B2: 1–6
Purity/Sanctity 5, 11 5, 11 B1: 1–5; B2: 1–6

Note: Block 1 items 6 (“good at math”) and Block 2 item 6 (“better to do good than bad”) are catch items excluded from scoring. Block 1 scores are rescaled to a 1–6 range before averaging with Block 2.

Beliefs about the Causes of Mental Illness (Knettel, 2019)

Attribution Items Scale
Social 1–5 (injustice, support, poverty, stress, trauma) 1–7
Biological 6–8 (genes, medical, chemical imbalance) 1–7
Personal 9–11 (will, choice, attitude) 1–7
Lifestyle 12–14 (diet, germs, activity) 1–7

Essentialist Beliefs about Addiction (GENBELIEFS3)

Subscale Items Scale
Categorical Thinking 1–11 1–5 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
Biological Essentialism 12–15 1–5

10.1.5 Chapter 7: Sociocultural Beliefs

Individualism vs. Collectivism (Triandis & Gelfand, 1998)

Orientation Items Scale
Horizontal Individualism 1, 4, 5, 6 1–9
Vertical Individualism 7, 8, 9, 10 1–9
Horizontal Collectivism 11, 12, 13, 14 1–9
Vertical Collectivism 15, 16, 17, 18 1–9

Lay Beliefs about Competition (Murayama & Elliot, 2012)

Subscale Items Scale
Performance 1–3 1–6
Character 4–6 1–6
Innovation 7–9 1–6
Unethical Behavior 10–12 1–6
Self-Harm 13–15 1–6
Social Harm 16–18 1–6

Social Identity Perceptions (16 items)

Scale: 1 (Hurts a Lot) to 5 (Helps a Lot), with 3 = Neither Helps nor Hurts.

10.1.6 Chapter 8: Health Behaviors

Social Media Addiction Scale (5 items)

Scale: 1 (Totally Disagree) to 7 (Totally Agree). Higher scores = more problematic use.

Health Behavior Profile (single items)

Item Scale
Life Satisfaction 1–6 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
Sleep (hrs/night) 0–10+
Sleep Quality 0–4 (Never to Very Refreshed)
Exercise (days/week) 0–7
Stress 0–10
Social Media (hrs/day) 0–10+
Food Security 1–4
Health Status 1–5 (Poor to Excellent)
Mental Health Status 1–5 (Poor to Excellent)
