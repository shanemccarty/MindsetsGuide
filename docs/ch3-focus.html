3  Regulatory Focus

This chapter examines three measures of regulatory focus — motivational orientations that shape whether you focus on pursuing positive outcomes (promotion) or avoiding negative outcomes (prevention). Understanding your regulatory focus orientation is foundational to health communication because it directly influences how you frame health messages and how those messages are received by different audiences.

3.1 Regulatory Focus Questionnaire

Constructs

The Regulatory Focus Questionnaire (RFQ; Higgins et al., 2001) measures whether your general behavioral style is driven by promotion or prevention.

  • Promotion Focus: An eagerness-based orientation toward achieving gains, pursuing ideals, and accomplishing aspirations
  • Prevention Focus: A vigilance-based orientation toward avoiding losses, fulfilling duties, and meeting obligations

Measures

The RFQ contains 11 items (6 promotion, 5 prevention) rated on a 5-point scale: 1 = Never or Seldom, 2 = Rarely, 3 = Sometimes, 4 = Often, 5 = Very Often. Higher scores indicate a stronger orientation toward that focus.

Example items:

  • Promotion: “How often have you accomplished things that got you ‘psyched’ to work even harder?”
  • Prevention: “How often did you obey rules and regulations that were established by your parents?”

3.2 Health Regulatory Focus Scale

Constructs

The Health Regulatory Focus Scale (HRFS; Ferrer et al., 2017) measures whether you approach your own health with a health promotion focus or health prevention focus orientation.

  • Health Promotion Focus: Striving toward ideal health, feeling accomplished by healthy behaviors, and admiring healthy people
  • Health Prevention Focus: Worrying about illness, feeling anxious about health obligations, and fearing future health problems

Measures

The HRFS contains 12 items (6 promotion, 6 prevention) rated on a 6-point scale: 1 = Strongly Disagree, 2 = Disagree, 3 = Slightly Disagree, 4 = Slightly Agree, 5 = Agree, 6 = Strongly Agree. Higher scores indicate a stronger health-specific focus.

Example items:

  • Health Promotion: “I frequently imagine how I can achieve a state of ‘ideal health.’”
  • Health Prevention: “I often worry that I am not doing the best I can to improve my health.”

3.3 Prosocial Regulatory Focus Scale

Constructs

The Prosocial Regulatory Focus Scale (McCarty et al., 2026) measures whether your prosocial motivation is oriented toward promoting care for others or preventing harm to others.

  • Promoting Care: Focusing on making positive impacts, uplifting others, and helping people achieve well-being
  • Preventing Harm: Focusing on stopping harmful actions, taking preventive action, and protecting people from threats

Measures

The scale contains 10 items (5 promoting care, 5 preventing harm) rated on a 6-point scale: 1 = Strongly Disagree, 2 = Disagree, 3 = Slightly Disagree, 4 = Slightly Agree, 5 = Agree, 6 = Strongly Agree. Higher scores indicate a stronger prosocial orientation in that direction.

Example items:

  • Promoting Care: “I typically focus on making a positive impact on people.”
  • Preventing Harm: “I am generally good at stopping harmful actions from happening to people.”
