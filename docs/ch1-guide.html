1  A Guide for You

This guide is your companion throughout the semester. Each week, it presents your individual scores alongside the class distribution so you can reflect on who you are as a health communicator.

1.2 How to Use Your Mindsets

Throughout this guide, you will encounter color-coded callout boxes that connect your psychographic scores to different components of the course:

ImportantPsychographics

Red callout boxes highlight connections to your psychographic self-assessments. We complete these assessments to learn more about ourselves and build self-awareness — the foundation for effective health communication.

TipCase Studies

Green callout boxes highlight connections to case study analysis. Each week, we consider how these mindsets influence our responses during case study discussions and real-world examples.

WarningHealth Campaign

Orange callout boxes highlight connections to your health communication campaign. We consider how these mindsets influence our ability to create effective health messages and the ways other people will receive those messages.

1.3 How to Find Your Scores

At the end of the anonymous survey, you created a password (5 letters + 4 numbers). Each chapter contains a table where you can look up your password to find your composite scores on that week’s measures.

NoteSave Your Password

Your password is the key to accessing your individual scores throughout the semester. Keep it saved in your phone notes or on a sticky note. If you want to replace your current password with a new password, contact Dr. Shane McCarty.

1.4 How to Interpret Your Scores

Each measure in this guide produces a composite score — a single number that summarizes your responses on a set of related survey questions. To calculate it, we take all of your answers within a subscale and average them together. For example, if a subscale has 5 questions and you answered 3, 4, 5, 4, and 4, your composite score would be 4.0. This gives you one easy-to-read number instead of having to interpret each question individually.

Here is how to interpret your results:

  1. Find your score in the password lookup table
  2. Locate your score on the class density plot — the dashed vertical lines show the 33rd and 67th percentiles
  3. Compare where you fall relative to the distribution: Are you low, mid, or high on this measure?
  4. Reflect on what your score means for how you receive and design health messages
NoteThere Are No Right or Wrong Scores

These measures capture a range of psychological beliefs and orientations. The purpose is self-awareness, not evaluation. Different mindsets bring different strengths to health communication.

1.5 About the Data

The data in this guide comes from the Anonymous Beliefs Survey completed by students in PH322 (Communicating Health Information) and ANTH206 (Community Health Research Methods) in Spring 2026. By combining data from both classes, we create a larger reference sample for you to compare your scores against.

  • All participants: Visualizations show the full sample from both classes
  • PH322 students: Your password lookup tables show scores for PH322 students specifically
  • Composite scores: Calculated as the average of your responses to the items within each subscale (after adjusting any reverse-coded items so that all questions point in the same direction)
